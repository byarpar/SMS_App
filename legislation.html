Legislation and Guidance

Relevant Legislation

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

The GDPR is a regulation in EU law on data protection and privacy in the European Union and the European Economic Area. It also addresses the transfer of personal data outside the EU and EEA areas.

  • Applies to processing of personal data
  • Gives individuals more control over their personal data
  • Imposes strict rules on those hosting and processing personal data

Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)

COPPA is a United States federal law that imposes certain requirements on operators of websites or online services directed to children under 13 years of age, and on operators of other websites or online services that have actual knowledge that they are collecting personal information online from a child under 13 years of age.

  • Requires parental consent for collection of personal information from children
  • Mandates clear privacy policies
  • Prohibits requiring personal information for a child to participate in activities

Digital Services Act (DSA)

The DSA is a European Union law that aims to create a safer digital space where the fundamental rights of users are protected and to establish a level playing field for businesses.

  • Targets illegal content, transparent advertising, and disinformation
  • Imposes obligations on digital services to reduce harms
  • Strengthens oversight of platform algorithms

Best Practice Guidance

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each social media account
  • Enable two-factor authentication when available
  • Regularly review and update privacy settings
  • Be cautious about sharing personal information online
  • Report and block abusive or inappropriate content
  • Be aware of the digital footprint you're creating
  • Think critically about the information you consume online
  • Respect others' privacy and obtain consent before sharing content about them

Stay Informed
