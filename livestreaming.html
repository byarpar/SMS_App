Livestreaming Safety

What is Livestreaming?

Livestreaming is the act of broadcasting video content in real-time over the internet. While it can be a fun and engaging way to connect with others, it's important to understand the risks and how to stay safe.

Staying Safe While Livestreaming

  • Be aware of your surroundings and what's visible in your stream
  • Don't share personal information during your livestream
  • Use platform safety tools and moderation features
  • Be cautious about interacting with strangers in your livestream
  • Think before you stream - content can be recorded and shared by others
  • Set clear boundaries for what you're comfortable sharing on livestream
  • Know how to end a livestream quickly if needed

Popular Livestreaming Platforms

Twitch

Primarily used for gaming streams, but also hosts other content like music and talk shows.

Safety Features:

  • Chat moderation tools
  • Ability to ban or timeout users
  • Two-factor authentication for account security

YouTube Live

Integrated with YouTube, allowing creators to stream to their subscribers.

Safety Features:

  • Age restrictions for certain content
  • Chat moderation settings
  • Option to review chat messages before they appear

Instagram Live

Part of Instagram Stories, allowing users to broadcast to their followers.

Safety Features:

  • Ability to block and report users
  • Option to turn off comments
  • Control over who can view your livestream

Livestreaming Tips

Remember, once you start streaming, anyone could be watching. Always be mindful of your privacy and safety. If you ever feel uncomfortable during a livestream, don't hesitate to end it immediately.
